üìñ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

‚ùì How do I track a flight?
  1. Open the app and tap "I'm Flying"
  2. Enter your flight number (e.g., "AA123" or "Y4182")
  3. Tap "Go" to start tracking
  4. Grant location permission when prompted to see your real-time position
  5. The map will show your route and nearby points of interest

Demo Mode: Try entering "Y4182" to see a demo flight from Mexico City to Tijuana!

‚ùì Why isn't GPS working?

Check Location Permissions:

  • iOS: Settings ‚Üí Privacy & Security ‚Üí Location Services ‚Üí wheresTHAT ‚Üí "While Using the App"
  • Android: Settings ‚Üí Apps ‚Üí wheresTHAT ‚Üí Permissions ‚Üí Location ‚Üí "Allow only while using the app"

Other Tips:

  • Make sure Location Services are enabled on your device
  • Close and restart the app
  • Ensure you have a clear view of the sky (GPS works best outdoors)
  • On flights, GPS may not work until you're above 10,000 feet
‚ùì How do I use the AR (Augmented Reality) view?
  1. While tracking a flight or road trip, tap the üì∑ camera icon in the top right
  2. Grant camera permission when prompted
  3. Point your camera at the horizon or window
  4. AR labels will appear showing nearby landmarks and their distances
  5. Move your phone around to see different points of interest

Best Results: Use AR mode outdoors or by a window with a clear view

‚ùì How do I save a location?
  1. Tap any marker or point of interest on the map
  2. View the location details in the popup modal
  3. Tap "üíæ Save for Later"
  4. Access saved locations from the home screen by tapping "Saved Locations"

‚ùì Can I use this on international flights?

Yes! wheresTHAT works worldwide for flights over any country.

  • Works with any airline and flight number
  • Shows landmarks and cities in any country
  • Supports multiple languages for place names
  • Note: You may need Wi-Fi on the plane to load map data
‚ùì How accurate is the flight tracking?

Flight tracking is approximate and for entertainment/educational purposes only.

  • GPS tracking updates every 10-30 seconds
  • POI distances are calculated based on your GPS position
  • Not for navigation or safety-critical decisions
  • Always follow airline crew instructions
‚ùì How do I use driving mode?
  1. From the home screen, tap "On the Road"
  2. Grant location permission when prompted
  3. Start driving - the app tracks your route automatically
  4. Points of interest appear as you drive past them
  5. Tap any POI to learn more about it
  6. When finished, tap "End Trip" to save to your history
‚ùì How do I view my trip history?
  1. From the home screen, tap "Trip History"
  2. View all your past flights and road trips
  3. See statistics: duration, distance, POIs viewed
  4. Tap any trip to see the full route and places you discovered
